Directional Derivatives

Some definitions

  • point: $p = (p^1, \dots,p^n)$
  • vector: $v = \langle v^1,\dots,v^n \rangle = v^ie_i$
  • line @ $p$: $c(t) = (p^1 + tv^1, \dots, p^n+tv^n )$

The directional derivative of a function $f: R^n \to R$ at a point $p$ in direction $v$ is defined as

$$D_vf = \lim_{t\to 0} \frac{f(c(t))-f(p)}{t} = \dv{}{t}\eval{}_{t = 0} f(c(t))$$

Then by [[Chain rule]] we can compute

$$Dvf = \dv{c^i}{t}(0)\pdv{f}{x^i}(p)$$
