The proof of the [[Consistency of M-Estimators (uniform convergence)]] has two major parts:

  • converging the “\(y\)” values: by squeezing \(M(\theta_0)\) in between \(M(\hat{\theta}_n)\) and \(M_n(\hat{\theta}_n)\)
  • “\(y\)” values only get close near \(\theta_0\): well separated says that the only place where \(M\) is near

Converging the \(y\) values

I was originally imagining the first part of the proof using a full \(M(\cdot)\) by \(\theta\) graph but it might be easier to just imagine it as a straight line.

We can also think of \(M(\theta_0)\) as a distance from \(0\) and similarly for \(M_n(\hat{\theta}_n)\). We’re then saying that \(M(\theta_0)\)’s distance from \(0\) is less than \(M_n(\hat{\theta}_n)\)’s distance to \(0\). 1

Then we re-normalize as a distance from \(M(\hat{\theta}_n)\) which we’re actually interested in2 and say this distance is converging to \(0\) 3.

So now we’ve established that the “\(y\)” values have converged. What does this mean for our “\(x\)” aka our \(\theta\)’s?

\(y\)-values only converge when the \(\theta\)’s do

By the well separated condition that

\[\sup{\theta: d(\hat{\theta}_n, \theta)\geq \epsilon}\quad M(\theta) < M(\theta_0)\]

we have that only \(y\) values that close to the max can only do so if the \(\theta_n\)’s converge to \(\theta_0\) the true max.
